What Slate Legal Needs to Fix This

Phase 1: Quick Wins (1-2 weeks)

Set up basic tracking & optimize homepage messaging

  • Install Google Analytics 4 properly - Their current GA setup is outdated (still using Universal Analytics). This is critical because you can't improve what you can't measure, and employment law firms need to track which services generate the most inquiries.
  • Add clear value propositions to homepage - Currently just says "We make employment law work for you" which is vague. Add specific benefits like "Get employment law advice within 24 hours" or "Fixed-fee employment contracts from £X" to immediately communicate value.
  • Create simple lead magnets - Offer free employment law checklists or templates (like "Disciplinary Process Checklist" for employers) in exchange for email addresses. This works perfectly for their dual audience of employers and employees.

Phase 2: Foundation Building (1 month)

Build content marketing engine & improve conversion paths

  • Launch weekly employment law blog - Start with 1-2 posts per week covering common questions like "Can I be dismissed for...?" or "New employment law changes for 2024." This addresses their lack of fresh content and helps with SEO for local searches.
  • Set up email marketing automation - Create separate nurture sequences for employers vs employees since they have different pain points. Employers need compliance help, employees need rights protection guidance.
  • Optimize service pages for conversions - Their current "Services" pages are too generic. Add specific case studies, pricing guides, and clear next-step CTAs like "Get Your Free 15-Minute Consultation."

Phase 3: Scale & Optimize (Ongoing)

Expand reach & systematize client acquisition

  • Develop local SEO strategy - Target "employment lawyer Newport" and "employment law Wales" since they're geographically focused. Create location-specific content and get listed in local directories.
  • Launch referral program - Employment law generates natural referrals from HR consultants, accountants, and other professionals. Formalize this with a structured referral system and tracking.
  • Test paid advertising - Once tracking is solid, test Google Ads for high-intent searches like "unfair dismissal lawyer" or "employment tribunal representation." Start small with £500/month budget and scale what works.
